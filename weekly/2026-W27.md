## Connect the dots

In the **unregulated** world of international trade, a single **commodity** like rare earth minerals can become a **hostage situation**, where one nation's **credible threat** to cut off supply forces others to **concede** to unfair terms. This creates a **commitment problem**, since no country can promise not to weaponize resources later out of pure **spite**. The result is an **escalation spiral** of tariffs and counter-tariffs, pushing the global market away from any stable **equilibrium**—all while consumers bear the cost, with **razor-thin margins** and **leakage** of profits to black markets.
